The Cooperative

The Cooperative project reuses the Sha'an Harod cooperative complex, transforming an industrial skeleton into a flexible civic centre. The existing warehouses and grain silos become a foundation for local initiatives, including community workshops, a market hall and rehearsal rooms. By preserving the robust concrete frame, the project celebrates the site's heritage while inviting new programmes.

To enable adaptability, prefabricated concrete elements assemble on‑site to form new columns and beams. This modular system allows lightweight enclosures and mezzanines to be inserted, creating a variety of spatial configurations. Each insert can be reconfigured or removed over time, aligning with the evolving needs of the community.

The design is guided by a belief that architecture can empower people by providing spaces that evolve with them. The Cooperative becomes a social infrastructure that fosters interaction between local entrepreneurs, artists and residents while strengthening the connection between the kibbutz and surrounding villages.

Location: Ein Harod, Israel Type: Mixed‑use / Preservation Studio: Third‑year design studio, Technion – Israel Institute of Technology Collaboration: with Omri Nadav Lecturer: Arch. Irit and Nitzan
